# Private AI Transcription: Local vs Cloud Audio Notes

Audio is sensitive. Meetings can include customer details, personal stories, strategy, financial information, or ideas that are not ready to be shared. That is why private AI transcription deserves more attention than it usually gets.

When a tool says it uses AI transcription, the next question should be: where does the audio go?

## Cloud Transcription

Cloud transcription sends audio to remote servers for processing. This can be powerful because cloud models can be large, fast, and frequently improved. It can also make setup easier across devices.

The trade-off is trust. You need to understand:

- What is uploaded.
- How long audio is stored.
- Whether transcripts are retained.
- Whether humans can review data.
- Whether data is used for model training.
- How deletion works.

Cloud tools can be appropriate, but the terms should be easy to find and easy to understand.

## Local Transcription

Local transcription processes audio on your own device or within hardware you control. This can reduce unnecessary exposure, especially for sensitive notes.

The trade-offs are usually battery, heat, processing speed, and model size. Smaller local models may be less capable than large cloud models, and hardware design becomes more important.

## Hybrid Transcription

Many products may use a hybrid approach: some processing locally, some in the cloud, and sync for backups or cross-device access. Hybrid systems can be useful, but they need clear controls.

Good questions include:

- Can I choose what syncs?
- Can I keep sensitive recordings local?
- Are summaries encrypted?
- Can I delete local and cloud copies?
- Does the product explain this before checkout?

## What Just Summit Means by On-Device-First

Just Summit Headphones are being designed around on-device-first processing principles. That means the product direction starts from the assumption that sensitive audio should stay under user control where practical.

This is not a final technical specification. Just Summit is still pre-production, and final hardware and software details will be confirmed as the build progresses. But the direction matters because privacy is easier to design in early than bolt on later.

## The Buyer Rule

If a product records your world, privacy should be visible before you pay. You should not need to dig through vague pages to understand what happens to your audio.

Private AI transcription is not only a feature for lawyers or executives. It is useful for anyone whose spoken information deserves care.
