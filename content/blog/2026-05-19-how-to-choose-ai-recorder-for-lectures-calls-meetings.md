# How to Choose an AI Recorder for Lectures, Calls, and Meetings

The right AI recorder depends on the audio you actually need to remember. A student in lectures, a founder on investor calls, and a consultant on site visits all have different needs.

Use this checklist before choosing a tool.

## Start With Your Main Audio Source

If most of your audio is scheduled video calls, software meeting assistants may be enough. If your audio happens in rooms, hallways, lectures, trains, or podcasts, you may need a device or wearable workflow.

Do not buy for the perfect demo. Buy for the situation you face every week.

## Decide Between Full Capture and Marked Moments

Some situations need full-session recording. Others only need a single marked idea. A strong workflow should make both possible, because you cannot always know in advance which moment will matter.

Just Summit Headphones are being designed around that distinction: capture a full meeting when needed, or mark important moments as you listen.

## Look Beyond Transcription Accuracy

Accuracy matters, but it is not the whole product. Also check:

- Summary quality.
- Search.
- Export options.
- Speaker handling.
- Privacy settings.
- Battery life.
- How easy it is to start and stop.

## Match the Form Factor to the Context

Phones are convenient but distracting. Table recorders can be effective but visible. Meeting bots are useful online but limited elsewhere. Headphones are already part of many calls, lectures, and listening sessions.
