# Private Meeting Notes for Neurodivergent Professionals

Meeting notes can be personal. They may include reminders, uncertainty, processing notes, names, client details, and the context someone needs to do their work well.

For neurodivergent professionals, privacy matters because the notes may reveal more than the meeting itself.

This is general productivity content, not healthcare advice or diagnosis.

## Private Notes Support Honest Notes

If every note is automatically shared, people may stop writing what they actually need. Private notes can include:

- "Ask for clarification"
- "Check I understood this"
- "Potential risk"
- "Follow up before replying"

These notes are useful because they are honest.

## Meeting Tools Should Respect Context

Some meetings need team-visible notes. Others need personal recall. A good workflow separates:

- Shared decisions
- Personal reminders
- Sensitive audio
- Draft thoughts
- Follow-up actions

Just Summit's direction is personal audio recall first, with privacy and user control treated as product requirements.

## Search Can Reduce Reprocessing

Reprocessing a meeting from scratch takes energy. Searchable notes and summaries can make it easier to return to the useful part without scanning a long transcript.

That matters when the question is simple: "What did we agree?" or "What did the client say about timing?"

## Consent Still Matters

Recording expectations vary by context. A privacy-first product should not make users careless. It should make control, transparency, and responsible use easier.

Just Summit should keep saying this plainly: users are responsible for using recording features appropriately in their own setting.

## The Better Standard

Private meeting notes should help people work with less friction while respecting sensitive context. They should not force everyone into the same note-taking style.

That is the opportunity for neurodivergent-friendly meeting recall.
